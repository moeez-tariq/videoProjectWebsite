Interactive Media

The Interactive Media major at NYU Abu Dhabi is designed to prepare students for an increasingly technology-driven world, where interactive media is fundamental to communication, creativity, and collaboration. The program offers a broad range of courses that cover various aspects of interactive media, including design, programming, user experience, and storytelling.

Through hands-on, project-based learning, students are encouraged to experiment with new technologies and explore creative possibilities. They work on real-world projects that challenge them to think critically about the role of media and technology in society and develop innovative solutions to complex problems.

The program fosters a culture of collaboration, inclusivity, and diversity, which allows students from different backgrounds and interests to work together and learn from each other. Faculty members, who are experts in their fields, provide mentorship and guidance to students, helping them develop the skills and knowledge they need to succeed in the field of interactive media.

Graduates of the program are well-prepared to pursue a wide range of careers in the field of interactive media, including digital media production, user experience design, multimedia journalism, interactive storytelling, and game design. They are also equipped with the skills and knowledge necessary to adapt to a rapidly changing technological landscape and to become leaders in the field of interactive media.

Further Information

IM-UH 1010: Intro to IM

This introductory course provides students hands-on experience with screen and physical interaction design.

Major Requirements

The Interactive Media major consists of 12 courses. Students are required to take four foundation courses, a minimum of six elective courses, and a Capstone Project.
